What NIBEX is

Built from first principles. Scored without assumption.

Most business assessments start with assumptions — that the financial picture tells the whole story, that digital presence is the primary risk, that operational problems will surface themselves. NIBEX starts with none of these assumptions.

It works through every dimension of a business systematically — economic position, market context, revenue model, cost structure, stakeholder dependencies, operations, people, customer relationships, digital infrastructure, strategic clarity, and legal compliance — producing a scored sub-element for each, aggregated into dimension scores, weighted by sector, and combined into a single NIBEX score.

The score is comparable across businesses, across sectors, and across time. A score of 58 means something specific. A score that moves from 42 to 67 over six months means something demonstrable.

The dereliction protocol
Any sub-element where a business is in active breach of a legal obligation scores -1 — not zero, not pending, but a true negative integer. This triggers a dimension ceiling of 2, preventing other scores in the same dimension from masking the exposure. The ceiling lifts only when every dereliction in that dimension is resolved. A business cannot score its way past a compliance failure.
Modular assessment
NIBEX assessments are modular. A Foundations engagement covers the core dimensions. Standard adds economic and strategic layers. Complete covers all eleven. A partial NIBEX score is always clearly notated — showing which dimensions have been assessed and which remain pending. Every assessment is the start of a complete picture, not a compromise.
The scoring scale

Every sub-element. One consistent scale.

Dereliction
Active legal breach creating jeopardy. Triggers dimension ceiling.
0
Absent
Should exist but does not. No active harm.
1

Exists in name only. Would not survive scrutiny.
2
Basic
Foundation exists but is incomplete or fragile.
3
Functional
Works for current needs. Identifiable gaps.
4
Developed
Systematic. Holds under pressure or scrutiny.
5
Optimised
Best practice for this type and scale of business.
